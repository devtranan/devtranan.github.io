Ma Pi Leng Pass

    • Ma Pi Leng Pass is located on National Route 4C, within the territory of Pai Vi and Pai Lung communes, connecting Ha Giang city with the towns of Meo Vac and Dong Van. Dubbed one of the "four great mountain passes" of Vietnam, this pass is one of the most famous tourist destinations in Ha Giang.
    Ma Pi Leng is a 20 km long winding and treacherous road situated at an altitude of over 1,200 meters above sea level. Travelers can embark on an adventurous journey, navigating through the sharp curves between steep cliffs and deep ravines. Don’t forget to stop at the "death rock" on the mountainside to take stunning "Instagram-worthy" photos!
Place

Lung Cu Flagpole

    • The Lung Cu Flagpole is located at the top of Dragon Mountain, at an altitude of 1,470 meters above sea level. As one of the four extreme points of the country, the Lung Cu Flagpole in Ha Giang marks the northernmost point of Vietnam.
    The flagpole was renovated and rebuilt in 2010, featuring a red and yellow flag measuring 54 square meters, symbolizing the 54 ethnic groups of Vietnam, flying proudly at a height of over 33 meters. The Lung Cu Flagpole is designed in an octagonal shape and has eight stone reliefs at its base, depicting Dong Son drum patterns and the glorious historical milestones of the nation.
Place

Dong Van Old Street

    • Pho Co Dong Van is a district built in the 20th century in Dong Van town, Ha Giang province. It is not only home to many ethnic groups but also one of the cultural and architectural features that need to be preserved in Ha Giang.
    In the ancient town, there are about 40 houses over 100 years old standing close together, surrounded by rocky mountains. Here, visitors will have the opportunity to admire the peaceful, ancient beauty of the gray walls bathed in golden sunlight in the morning. At night, the scene transforms into a lively atmosphere filled with the sounds of mouth organs and the dance of young men and women from the village.
